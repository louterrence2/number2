How the Intrinsic Value Model Works

The model estimates a company's intrinsic value (market cap) at different coal price scenarios using a standard EV/EBITDA methodology. This shows how sensitive a company's value is to market price changes.

  1. Project EBITDA Change: It calculates the change in earnings by multiplying a company's annual production (tons) by the change in coal price from a baseline ($250/ton for Met, $130/ton for Thermal).
  2. Determine New Enterprise Value: The new projected EBITDA is multiplied by a standard industry valuation multiple (3.5x for coal) to find a new total company value (Enterprise Value).
  3. Find Final Market Cap: It subtracts the company's net debt from the Enterprise Value. If the result is negative, it means the company's debt would be greater than its entire value at that price, indicating a high risk of bankruptcy.
